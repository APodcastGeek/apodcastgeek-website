Episode Brief Generator

Use this page to create a completed Episode Brief before recording a podcast guest conversation.

🎯

The Episode Brief is designed to help the host walk into the conversation with clear intention, a strong strategic direction, and a defined listener journey. It is not just an interview question sheet. It is a pre-production document that connects the podcast's audience, the guest's expertise, and the purpose of the episode.

What this creates

The Episode Brief Generator helps you produce a finished brief that includes…
  • Podcast and episode details
  • Guest name and title
  • Episode Goal and North Star
  • The core purpose of the conversation
  • Why the episode matters to the audience
  • The single most important listener takeaway
  • Audience transformation from A to B
  • A clear value proposition for the episode
  • Guest bio and credibility points
  • A structured conversation map
  • Strategic interview questions
  • A host-ready guest intro, where required by the template

Why this matters

A strong podcast episode is not built around a random list of questions…

It is built around a clear strategic purpose. This process helps you:

  • Avoid generic conversations
  • Clarify what the episode needs to accomplish
  • Connect the guest's expertise to your audience's needs
  • Define what the listener should think, feel, understand, or do by the end
  • Give the host a stronger way into the conversation
  • Create better questions that uncover stories, examples, frameworks, and useful insights
  • Improve the quality of downstream content, including titles, clips, intros, and promotional copy

Tools you can use

This prompt works with ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or any advanced AI assistant…

For best results, use a tool that supports:

  • File uploads
  • Web research or browsing
  • Reading Word documents or Google Docs exports
  • Generating a downloadable document
  • Preserving document structure and formatting

Results may vary slightly depending on the AI tool you use. For the most accurate formatting, upload the APodcastGeek Episode Brief template as a .docx file and instruct the AI to use it as the design shell rather than recreating the document from scratch.

What you need before starting

Prepare the following before using the prompt…
  • Your Podcast Guide doc (sent via email after our initial design call)
  • The APodcastGeek Episode Brief template
  • Guest name
  • Guest LinkedIn URL
  • Guest website, if available
  • Any extra notes you already have about the guest
♻️

The Podcast Guide gives the AI context about your show, audience, tone, themes, positioning, and goals.

The Episode Brief template gives the AI the structure and visual format it needs to preserve.

The guest details allow the AI to research the guest and suggest strategic episode directions.

Important formatting instruction

⚠️

Do not ask the AI to create a new document from scratch. Ask it to use the uploaded Episode Brief template as the design shell. The AI should preserve: logo placement, page layout, heading structure, boxed North Star section, borders, section order, font hierarchy, spacing style, bullet formatting, and numbered question formatting.

The AI should replace only the placeholder content with completed content.

How the workflow works

1
Upload the Podcast Guide +

Upload your APodcastGeek Podcast Guide. The AI should read this first so it understands the purpose of the podcast, target audience, audience problems, desires, beliefs and objections, the tone and personality of the show, the host's point of view, recurring themes, and what makes a strong episode for this audience.

2
Upload the Episode Brief template +

Upload the APodcastGeek Episode Brief template. The AI should use this as the document shell and preserve the structure as closely as possible.

3
Provide guest details +

Give the AI: guest name, guest LinkedIn URL, guest website if available, and any extra context you have.

4
Let the AI research the guest +

The AI researches the guest using the information provided, looking for: current role, background, career story, expertise, strong points of view, notable achievements, relevant projects, credentials, and topics they are credible to speak about.

If LinkedIn cannot be fully accessed, the AI should use the guest's website and other reliable public sources. It should not invent facts.

5
Choose an Episode Strategy Direction +

Before creating the final brief, the AI generates five Episode Strategy Directions. Each includes a direction title, North Star, core purpose, why the episode matters, Audience A, Audience B, value proposition, and why the guest fits.

This is the most important step. You are not just choosing a topic. You are choosing the strategic spine of the episode.

6
Approve, refine, combine, or ask for more +

After seeing the five directions, reply with: "I choose option 2", "Refine option 4", "Combine options 1 and 3", or "Generate five more". The AI should not create the completed brief until you approve one direction. Repeat until the direction feels right.

7
Generate the completed Episode Brief +

Once a direction is approved, the AI completes the full Episode Brief using the uploaded template, including: episode goal and North Star, core purpose, why the episode matters, single most important takeaway, audience A to B transformation, value proposition, guest bio, conversation map, 10 to 12 interview questions, and guest intro where required.

🔍 What good output looks like

  • Specific to the guest
  • Aligned with the Podcast Guide
  • Clear about the listener journey
  • Built around one strong North Star
  • Useful for the host before recording
  • Structured without feeling scripted
  • Practical for the target audience
  • Written in a finished, professional style
  • Formatted in the APodcastGeek template

Quality checklist before recording

Before using the brief, check…
  • Is the North Star clear?
  • Does the episode have a clear listener A to B transformation?
  • Does the guest fit the chosen direction?
  • Will the audience care about this conversation?
  • Are the questions specific and useful?
  • Does the Conversation Map create a natural arc?
  • Does the brief help the host lead a stronger conversation?
  • Does the final document preserve the APodcastGeek template structure?

Provide it as a downloadable Google Doc-style document or .docx file. Do not provide a copy/paste text version unless I specifically ask for one.

The Episode Brief is not a formality. It is the strategic foundation of the conversation. Use it to decide what the episode is really trying to achieve before you press record.
