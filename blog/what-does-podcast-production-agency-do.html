What Does a Podcast Production Agency Do? The Complete APG Breakdown

What Does a Podcast Production Agency Do?

The short answer: everything except hit record and show up.

A full-service podcast production agency handles the entire ecosystem around your podcast. From pre-production strategy through post-production delivery and distribution, we manage the operational complexity so you can focus on what you do best: having great conversations.

At APodcastGeek, we've been recognised at the Irish Podcast Awards for our approach to this work. But rather than speak in generalities, let's be specific about what we actually deliver and what clients never have to think about again.

The Complete Deliverables Per Episode

When you work with us through our APG Brand Builder service, here's exactly what you receive for each episode:

1. Video Podcast

A professionally edited, full-length video version of your podcast optimised for YouTube and YouTube Shorts. We handle camera setup, multi-angle filming, graphics overlays, colour grading, and final export in multiple formats.

2. Audio Podcast

A mastered audio file ready for distribution across all major platforms (Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Amazon Music, etc.). This includes noise reduction, EQ balancing, normalisation, and loudness optimisation to meet platform specifications.

3. Guest Introduction Trailer

A 30-60 second video clip that introduces your guest and teases the episode. This is optimised for LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Instagram. Here's an example of what we produce:

4. Six Short-Form Clips

Six 15-60 second clips extracted from the full episode, designed for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Each clip is edited to stand alone, with captions, B-roll, and graphics applied. These clips are optimised for each platform's specifications and best-performing formats.

5. Three Custom Thumbnails

Three variations of episode thumbnails for YouTube, each A/B/C tested and designed according to YouTube's current best practices for click-through rates. We use data-backed design principles rather than guesswork.

6. Show Notes

Comprehensive show notes including a summary, timestamp markers, speaker bios, relevant links, and key takeaways. These are formatted for both your podcast hosting platform and your website.

7. SEO Article

A 1,200-1,500 word blog post built around the episode content, optimised for search engines and your target keywords. This includes internal linking, featured images, and proper heading structure to drive organic traffic.

8. Full Distribution

We handle submission to all major podcast platforms. You don't manually upload anywhere. We manage the entire distribution workflow, ensuring consistency across channels.

That's eight distinct deliverables per episode. Most agencies do one or two. We do all eight.

The Pre-Production Briefing Process

Quality output depends on clear input. We don't wing this part.

Before any recording happens, both the host and guest receive detailed, customised briefs. These aren't generic templates. Each brief is tailored to the episode topic, the guest's background, and your business objectives.

Host Brief Includes

  • Episode theme and key topics to cover
  • Guest background and relevant talking points
  • Suggested questions designed to create quotable moments
  • Technical setup requirements (lighting, audio, camera position)
  • Best practic es for on-camera presence and pacing

Guest Brief Includes

  • Overview of your podcast and typical listener profile
  • Episode context and theme
  • Key messages the host wants to explore
  • Technical setup guidance
  • Tips for creating shareable moments

This investment in pre-production reduces recording friction and increases the likelihood of creating content worth extracting into six separate clips. It's the difference between a meandering conversation and a strategic narrative.

The 10 Business Day Turnaround

Once recording is complete, we deliver all eight deliverables within 10 business days. This timeline assumes you've provided finished video and audio files from your recording.

What happens in those 10 days:

  • Day 1-2: Ingestion, logging, and initial quality check
  • Day 2-4: Video editing, colour correction, graphics
  • Day 3-5: Audio mastering and format export
  • Day 4-6: Clip extraction and short-form video editing
  • Day 5-7: Thumbnail design and iteration
  • Day 6-8: Show notes and SEO article writing
  • Day 8-10: Distribution setup and final delivery

These workstreams run in parallel, not sequentially. Our production workflow is designed for efficiency without cutting quality.
