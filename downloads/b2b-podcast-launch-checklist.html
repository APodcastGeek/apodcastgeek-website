The B2B Podcast Launch Checklist

7 decisions every B2B founder needs to make before launching a podcast that generates pipeline and revenue.

This checklist covers the strategic foundations, not the technical setup. Get these 7 decisions right and the rest follows. Get them wrong and you'll burn time and money on a podcast that sounds good but delivers nothing. Work through each section, tick the boxes, and bring this to your strategy call.

1Target Listener Profile

Your podcast is not for everyone. Define exactly who you are creating this for. The more specific, the more effective.

2Show Format

The right format depends on your goals. Interview shows build relationships with guests (who become prospects). Solo shows build personal authority. Most B2B founders benefit from interview-led formats.

3Episode Cadence

Consistency matters more than frequency. A bi-weekly podcast you sustain for 12 months beats a weekly podcast you abandon after 8 episodes.

4Guest Strategy

Your guest list is your prospect list. On average, 10% of podcast guests convert to customers or long-term partnerships. Choose guests strategically.

5Distribution Plan

Publishing on Spotify and Apple is the minimum. For B2B, YouTube and LinkedIn are where your audience actually lives.

6Content Repurposing Engine

One podcast episode should generate a minimum of 10 content assets. If you are only publishing the episode and moving on, you are leaving 90% of the value on the table.

7Success Metrics

Downloads are a vanity metric for B2B. Measure what matters: relationships, pipeline, and revenue.
